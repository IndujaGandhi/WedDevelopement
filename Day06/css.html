like light that learns to linger where I go.

Two quiet hands that know the map of mine,
we trace the edges till the nights align.

— Arin Vale

Say my name like summer says the shore,
and all the wild returns, wanting more.

— L. H. Rivers

Your laugh bends space between our days,
folds the distance into simpler ways.

— Noor Iqbal

I keep a small room in me for only you —
a window, a glass of moonlight, and a view.

— C. R. Hale

If time could be a ribbon, I would tie
our days in loops so none could say goodbye.

— S. K. Juno
